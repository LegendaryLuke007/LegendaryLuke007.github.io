## Chess Club:

During my Junior year of high school, I witnessed the "spring break that never ended" with the onslaught of the Covid-19 Pandemic during Spring of 2020. This forced me to collect some new hobbies, and one that I collected was chess. When college came around, I noticed that there was no dedicated Chess club at the University of Alaska Fairbanks, so I took it upon myself to foster a local chess comunnity. Initally it was buying 4 roll-up chess boards with clocks, setting them up in Arctic Java (central cafe on campus in the Wood Center), and trying to spread the word of a consistent "chess meet" where anyone was welcome to show up and just play. After the first year, opening a discord server, collecting names and learning how to spread the word, we got a consistent 8-10 members every week showing up to play. During the Second year we submitted a team of players to play competitively every weekend against other collegiate chess clubs through the Collegiate Chess League by [Chess.com](https://www.chess.com/). This added a level of competitive energy that we hadn't witnessed before in the group, which pushed me to organize and gather funding for the 2022 Pan American Intercollegiate Chess League in Seattle. This was a lot of organizing almost exclusivley on my shoulders (as the President and main driving force of the club). But we managed to secure a small amount of funding for the trip (some local sponsorships and University funds), organized the few people who needed help paying for flights, and we all threw in money to pay for an Airbnb a few blocks from the competition. This competition was a highlight of my college carrer. It wasn't only a fun experience to be there and to compete against other schools (our school also won individuals "Surprise Upset" award and the "Surprise Upset" award as a team), but it also showed me that I was quite good at planning and then executing on a project with multiple people and organizations involved. It was my first real life "test" to see how effective of a leader I could be.
